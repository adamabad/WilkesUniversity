IPv6 is the response to the explosive boom in need of internet addresses. IPv6 uses 128 bits, represented in a series of hexadecimal values to produce 42 undecillion new available addresses. (1) Because of the inaccessibility of changing older devices to meet the new protocol, both IPv4 and IPv6 work in tandem. Together, these two protocols govern all internet traffic.
