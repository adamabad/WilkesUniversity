Prev | Next | IPv4 vs. IPv6

IPv4 is the most widely used set of addresses worldwide. Officially, IPv4 has run out of available addresses because of its size limitations. The protocol uses 32 bits to assign a unique address to every device. This, however, can only fit 4.3 billion unique addresses. With the introduction of smartphones and IoT (internet of things), the use of the internet has boomed, quickly outpacing the availability of space. The counter this, IPv6 was implemented and is slowly replacing the existing IPs.
